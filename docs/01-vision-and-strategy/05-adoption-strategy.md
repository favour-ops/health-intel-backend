# Adoption Strategy

## How Does a Private Platform Become National Infrastructure?

The real question: How do you get 5,000 hospitals to join without making it mandatory?

**Answer: Make it so valuable that NOT joining costs them more.**

---

## Phase 1: Proof of Concept (Months 1-3)

### Target: 5 Hospitals in 1 City

**Goal:** Prove the concept works and is valuable.

**Why 1 city?**
- Easier coordination
- Faster feedback
- Visible results
- Media attention (local news)

**Why 5 hospitals?**
- Enough to show impact
- Enough to test real-time coordination
- Enough to demo at scale

**Incentive:**
- Free to hospitals
- Direct visibility into their own capacity
- Proof they're "high-tech"
- Good for accreditation

**Success:**
- Hospitals see value (faster planning, reduced chaos)
- Citizens see value (faster emergency routing)
- City government sees value (health system efficiency)
- Media covers it: "Smart hospital system reduces emergency delays by 25%"

---

## Phase 2: City Expansion (Months 4-9)

### Target: All Hospitals in Lagos (Example)

**Goal:** Prove the concept at city-wide scale.

**Incentive:**
- State government endorsement
- Accreditation requirement (state mandate)
- Public funding support
- Citizens trust it (government backing)

---

## Phase 3: Regional Expansion (Months 9-18)

### Target: 5 States

**Goal:** Prove regional coordination works.

**New Incentive:**
- Insurance companies integrate with Health Intel
- Faster reimbursements for hospitals on platform
- Insurance customers can see hospital capacity
- Hospitals outside system get slower reimbursements

**Insurance Leverage:**
- Insurance pays for 40% of healthcare in Nigeria
- If insurance says "join Health Intel", hospitals join
- This is the **economic enforcement mechanism**

---

## Phase 4: National Scale (Year 2+)

### Target: All 36 States

**Goal:** Become the official health data source.

**At this point, joining is mandatory because:**

1. **Government policy**
   - Health Ministry requires it for licensure
   - Accreditation boards require it

2. **Economic incentives**
   - Can't get government contracts without it
   - Can't get insurance reimbursements quickly without it
   - Can't access emergency funding without it

3. **Public pressure**
   - Citizens demand hospitals on the platform
   - "This hospital isn't on the health intelligence system"
   - Becomes a credential

4. **International requirements**
   - WHO reporting requires this data
   - Development partners require transparency
   - Donor funding is conditioned on this
